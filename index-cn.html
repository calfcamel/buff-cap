About Buff Cap Calculator

This website aims to assist you with calculating your buff cap in World of Warcraft: Classic.
What is the buff cap?
In WOW Classic, you can only have 32 buffs on your character at once. Exceeding this number means that the oldest buff will vanish. Invariably, this is one of your world buffs like Slip'kik's Savvy . To prevent important buffs from disappearing, strict rationing of buffs is required. This website should help with that.
Who is responsible for this?

This website was originally written by Querns. I'm on Fairbanks (Horde) as "Querns" if you want to send me some gold. The tool was adapted from a body of work from the Fight Club discord chat server, but quickly grew beyond its warrior focus to encompass as much information about every class as I could gather.

Arcane Might

Arcane Might is a buff available from Crimson Sorcerers in Stratholme (Live Side.)

Acquiring Arcane Might requires unusual action. Priests employing their Mind Control ability CANNOT cast this buff. In order to acquire this buff, a 侏儒洗脑帽 must be employed. Use it on the Crimson Sorcerer, and wait a few seconds. The NPC should buff you with the ability shortly.

The exact mechanics in play here are not known to me at this time. If I had to guess, it is because of the pet bar UI. Crimson Sorcerers have five abilities , and the pet bar only has room for four. Even though the pet bar UI is used when a priest uses his or her Mind Control ability, direct movement control of the NPC is granted to the priest. This prevents the NPC from auto-casting its abilities. Conversely, a Gnomish Mind Control cap makes the NPC your pet, and it is free to auto-cast its abilities.

UPDATE 2020/05/26: Testing has been performed, and it is confirmed that Priests are neither able to use a macro to force the mind controlled mob to cast the ability, nor are they able to manually type a /cast command to force the spell cast. Big thanks to Rivx of Razorgore (EU) for doing the testing for this. (video proof)
